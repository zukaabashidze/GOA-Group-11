Overview

World War I, also known as the Great War, began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria. It lasted until 1918 and involved many of the world's great powers.

Causes

The causes of World War I are complex and multifaceted, including nationalism, militarism, alliances, and imperialism.

Major Battles

  • Battle of the Somme
  • Battle of Verdun
  • Battle of Gallipoli

Aftermath

The aftermath of World War I saw significant political, cultural, and social changes across the world, including the Treaty of Versailles and the establishment of the League of Nations.
