Overview

World War II, which lasted from 1939 to 1945, was the most widespread and deadliest conflict in history, involving more than 30 countries and resulting in significant global impact.

Causes

The causes of World War II include the Treaty of Versailles, the rise of totalitarian regimes, economic instability, and aggressive expansionism by Germany, Japan, and Italy.

Major Battles

  • Battle of Stalingrad
  • Battle of Midway
  • Battle of Normandy (D-Day)

Aftermath

The aftermath of World War II saw the establishment of the United Nations, the beginning of the Cold War, and significant political, economic, and social changes worldwide.
